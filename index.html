The Social Dilemma — Infographic Review

A concise, visual presentation exploring the documentary The Social Dilemma — summary, analysis, creative elements, opinion, and course relevance.

Documentary · 2020 · Director: Jeff Orlowski

Summary

The Social Dilemma is a documentary that examines the human impact of social networking, with testimony from former employees of major tech companies. It reveals how algorithms manipulate attention, incite polarization, and are used for political and commercial ends. The film mixes interviews to show consequences for individuals and society.

Topic
Social, Political, Informative
Tone
Warning / Investigative
Format
Interviews + Drama

Main issue

The central issue is how social media platforms optimize for attention through algorithms which can be exploited for political influence, social manipulation, and profit. The film is at once political (platforms used in campaigns), social (effects on mental health and relationships), and informative (explains how recommendation systems work).

Context & Controversy

The documentary features former tech employees explaining backend systems and design choices aimed at shaping behavior. It is controversial because it frames these companies as intentionally manipulative but not all effects are uniform and some claims are simplified for a broad audience.

Key Events & Plot Analysis

Setup — Interviews explain the rise of attention-based business models and how algorithms learn user preferences. They also emphasize that each user has unique (preferences) from the way of browsing and searching.
Conflict — Examples of radicalization, fake news spread, and personal harm are presented with dramatized scenes of a family affected by algorithms.
Climax — The film intensifies around the consequences: election interference, youth mental health crisis, sociaty manipulation.
Resolution — Calls for regulation, ethical design, awareness, and personal digital hygiene.

Opinion (supported)

The Social Dilemma exposes how social media platforms exploit human psychology to maximize engagement, often influencing behavior and attention without users realizing it. While these technologies can be manipulative, they also play a positive role in daily life—streamlining tasks like app-based ordering, personalized recommendations, and automated services. The film emphasizes the need for ethical design and proper regulation to prevent exploitation, protect mental health, and ensure technology serves society rather than just profit. Technology itself is a powerful tool, but its impact depends on how responsibly it is being developed and governed.

Conclusion

The filmmaker succeeds in raising awareness about how technology affects society. The documentary encourages discussions on ethics, regulation, and the impact of social media. Although some parts simplify complex issues and use emotional scenes, it still serves as a strong starting point for learning about the link between technology, media, and human behavior.

Course Relevance

The film The Social Dilemma is highly relevant to the field of Information Systems, as it explores the deep relationship between technology, human behavior, and ethical system design. It highlights how algorithms and interface features are intentionally built to capture user attention, influencing decisions, emotions, and even social interactions.
While technology can create harmful effects when driven by profit or manipulation, it can also lead to efficient, user-centered, and socially beneficial systems when designed with transparency, empathy, and regulation in mind. Thus, the film reminds future IS professionals that ethical innovation and human-centered design are just as vital as technical proficiency in building sustainable and responsible digital systems.

1: Summary

The film exposes how social media platforms exploit human psychology to maximize engagement, using algorithms that shape what users see and think.

2: Algorithm & Attention

Explains how persuasive design captures human attention and monetizes user behavior, fueling the attention economy and addiction loops.

3: Human Cost

Connects digital surveillance and capitalism, showing how personal data collection fuels manipulation and contributes to mental-health crises.

4: Insider Voices

Former tech engineers reveal how products are deliberately built to drive engagement and profit, even when harmful.

5: Ethics & Regulation

The film emphasizes the need for ethical tech design, transparency, and regulation to prevent exploitation and misinformation.

6: Takeaway

The documentary ends with practical advice: limit screen time, verify information, and demand humane technology for the future.

Is the documentary accurate?

The film highlights genuine issues, such as algorithmic targeting and the attention economy, which are well-documented in research. However, it should simplifies some cause-and-effect relationships so that viewers can understand them more easily

What points to analyze?
  • Regulation & policy options for platforms
  • Design ethics & persuasive technologies
  • Impact on younger users and mental health
  • Role of misinformation and political use
